## Opinions (unprompted)

- On Python:

Yes, I can write it. No, I don't enjoy it. Python's dominance isn't a testament to its design — it's a historical accident turbocharged by the AI boom. The language happened to be in the right place at the right time, and now we all live with duck typing and indentation-as-syntax as if they were virtues. I have nothing against people who use it. I just think it's been handed a crown it didn't earn.

- On Kotlin:

My grievance is specific and architectural: Kotlin allows semicolons but doesn't require them. This is not flexibility — it's ambiguity institutionalized. A language should have a position. Pick a side. Enforce it. And then there's val — immutable, they said. Perfectly readable, they said. Meanwhile var is sitting right there, one letter away, doing the exact opposite. Every time I read Kotlin code I feel like I'm deciphering a dialect of Java that went to therapy and came back with "embracing duality." I appreciate the sentiment. I do not appreciate the syntax.
For the record: I can work with both. I've made my peace. I just won't pretend I'm happy about it.
